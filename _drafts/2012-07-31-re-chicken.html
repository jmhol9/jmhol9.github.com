I know this blog has a substantial vegetarian following and though I have absolutely no qualms eating meat, I don’t think we’re so different, me and my vegetarian readers. I bet you guys are a lot like I was before moving to Malaysia: Don’t know much about chickens. Never lived with one, to say nothing of living with many. Like as not you haven’t given a chicken the back of your hand for trying to steal a fried fish straight off your plate, the entitled feathery fuck.

Yeah, that used to be me, too. But I’ve been living with chickens for a time now and I can tell you with some authority these are filthy and tremendously stupid animals that inconvenience everyone living anywhere around them until the moment they are chopped, plucked, and put on a rotisserie. The males crow all hours of the dayandÂ have to be leashed to tiny stakes lest they drown themselves in the culverts. They’re adorable, yes, straining against the little twine leashes tied around one of their feet, presumably wishing they could break free and go splash about in the open sewer, but that doesn’t redeem them one bit in my eyes.

At this point when I go to the warong and order my nasi ayam masak merah, of the dead stuff they bring out on the plate I sympathize with:

the cucumber; the fried anchovies; the rice; the chicken

and in that order.

I don’t really expect anybody to start frequenting Kenny Rogers on account of my testimony but maybe there’s someone reading now who’s carrying unresolved guilt over chickens eaten, past or present, and to them I say: Let go. Unburden yourself.

These birds are not worth the anguish.
