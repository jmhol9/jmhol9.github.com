The advantages of chucking your infant child the first time it causes you any grief are too obvious to need listing.

But are there disadvantages, too? Yes in fact. They’re just easy to overlook, which is why the Malaysian government has printed these enlightening public service announcements detailing the perils of tossing out your baby.

Dead/unhealthy baby. Guilt. Dangerous overnourishment of the nation’s stray animal population. Don’t rush into discarding your baby behind the nearest goat curry stall without first figuring these cons into your decision making process.

On a more serious note, what does it say about a country when its people need PSAs convincing them not to abandon their babies? Or, which I bet is the more likely case here, when its government inexplicably thinks the people need such convincing? Families here must average eight, maybe nine kids.
